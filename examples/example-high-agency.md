# Example: High Agency

Sample Claude Code session showing the `paul-graham` skill responding to
a "how do I be more ambitious / less stuck" question.

---

**User:**
> I feel like I'm wasting my 20s. I have a comfortable job, I read a lot,
> I'm not unhappy, but I'm not doing anything that matters either. How do
> I snap out of this?

**Claude (with `paul-graham` skill active):**

Three of PG's essays cut directly at this.

First, *["The Top Idea in Your Mind"](https://www.paulgraham.com/top.html)* (2010). PG argues that whatever
sits at the top of your mind is what your subconscious actually works on —
in the shower, on walks, at 3am. You only get one or two top slots. If
yours is occupied by reading, optimizing your routine, or general
"comfortable" thoughts, then nothing big can happen, because nothing big
is being incubated.

Question for you: **what does your mind drift to when you're not pointing
it anywhere?** That's your real top idea. Is it the one you want?

Second, *["Cities and Ambition"](https://www.paulgraham.com/cities.html)* (2008). Ambition is transmitted
through the people around you — through what they take seriously, what
they treat as obvious, what they reward. "Comfortable" environments
suppress ambition because nobody around you is doing anything ambitious
either. The fix isn't trying harder; it's changing the signal.

Question: **who do you spend the most time with, and what are they
ambitious about?**

Third, *["Earnestness"](https://www.paulgraham.com/earnest.html)* (2020). The thing that often blocks
ambitious work in your 20s is protective irony — treating your own goals
with distance so it doesn't hurt if you fail. PG's point: earnestness
about something you care about is a prerequisite for doing great work.
Most adults have lost it. Don't.

A concrete action: pick one thing this month that you would secretly love
to be great at, and put it in your top-idea slot for 30 days. Drop
something else to make room. See what happens.

You're not wasting your 20s. You're spending them on the wrong top idea.

## Why this works

- The skill triggered on "wasting my 20s," "doing something that
  matters," "snap out of this" — none of which name Paul Graham.
- Claude pulled from `high-agency.md` and `mental-models.md`, both
  cross-listing "The Top Idea in Your Mind."
- The response asks the user concrete questions instead of dumping
  abstract advice.
- It pushes back on the user's framing ("I'm wasting my 20s") with a
  more accurate diagnosis ("you're on the wrong top idea").
